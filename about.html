Our Story

A Collaborative Project, Built with Intention

The Magick Muse is a collaborative project built by my husband and me, a living grimoire designed to make witchcraft knowledge organized, accessible, and searchable for modern practitioners. I lead the research and content, and he handles the technical development and design, so the project can grow into the structured, community-centered resource it was always meant to be.

A Decade of Practice
My Roots in the Craft

The Craft Has Been in My Life Longer Than My Practice Has

I've been a practicing witch for about a decade, but the craft has been in my life much longer than that. The women in my family have read tarot and practiced small, everyday forms of spellwork for as long as I can remember. Growing up surrounded by that tradition shaped my relationship with magick long before I ever began studying it formally on my own.

My personal practice is rooted in working with plants, candles, tarot, runes, and crystals, with a strong focus on protection magick, both personal and for collective well-being.

Why a Digital Grimoire?

Paper Is Finite. The Craft Is Not.

What began as my personal grimoire slowly evolved into something much larger, and it started with a very real frustration.

I'm autistic, and a traditional handwritten grimoire simply wasn't going to work for me. I tried, over and over. I'd erase what I wrote because my handwriting didn't look right, or I'd put something in the wrong spot and end up throwing away the whole page. But beyond the physical limitations, there was something deeper: paper is finite. A digital grimoire lets me expand in all directions as I learn, as I read new books, encounter new ideas, and make new connections. Nothing gets siloed. Nothing gets lost. And being able to search instead of digging through a stack of books, especially mid-ritual, has been genuinely life-changing.

The Magick Muse was built to reflect those relationships through careful organization and cross-referencing. Every entry is part of a larger system, designed to help practitioners find information quickly and understand how different elements of magick relate to each other.

Growing Into Something Bigger

A Living Grimoire That Grows With the Community

Sharing this work publicly changed everything. The response made it clear that other practitioners needed exactly this.

🌱

Built to Be Shared

As a teacher by nature, sharing what I know has always come naturally to me. When I first shared a glimpse of this grimoire online, the response from the community was overwhelming, and that encouragement inspired me to expand the project and open it up to others.

100,000+ Refinements in Six Months

In just the first six months of sharing it publicly, I made over 100,000 updates, additions, and refinements. New information and insights are constantly being incorporated. This is a living grimoire. It grows, evolves, and deepens over time.

My Philosophy

Good Information. Vetted Information. The Kind You Can Trust.

From the very beginning, I was intentional about where my information came from. Every entry in the Magick Muse is sourced from published, reputable books, not random corners of the internet, not rumors passed from person to person. I wanted good information. Vetted information. The kind you can actually trust.

Yes, I recognize the irony of building an online resource while being skeptical of online sources. But that tension is exactly the point.

We're in a moment where many new practitioners are learning the craft entirely through social media and unverified posts, without the anchoring that comes from real study or lived tradition. That worries me. Without trustworthy sources, it's easy to unknowingly do things that are harmful, using the wrong correspondences, calling upon spirits without proper context, or appropriating closed practices without realizing it.

The Magick Muse exists, in part, to offer an alternative. A place where practitioners, especially those just starting out, can explore with confidence, knowing that what they're reading has been carefully researched and is rooted in legitimate sources. My hope is that this gives people peace of mind, and helps the craft be practiced with the care and respect it deserves.
